## Summary

Replace the global, static presentation level (derived from age alone) with a per-subject adaptive distribution. Each student has weighted probabilities across presentation levels per subject (e.g., 10% primary, 75% intermediate, 15% standard). Performance nudges the distribution's center — success at higher levels pulls it forward, failure at lower levels pushes it back. The diagnostic seeds the initial distribution. Content selection samples from it. No manual overrides — the system navigates students to the right level through observation and adaptation.

## Phase 3: Distribution Drift Engine
**Goal:** Session performance continuously nudges the presentation distribution with asymmetric update rules. Students are gradually pushed toward higher linguistic complexity.

1. [ ] [IMP] Build `nudgeDistribution(distribution, servedLevel, success)` pure function: applies a small delta based on which level was served and whether the student succeeded. Asymmetric rules:
   - Success at center level: small nudge toward next level up (+0.02 to level above, -0.02 from center)
   - Failure at center level: small nudge toward next level down (+0.02 to level below, -0.02 from center)
   - Success above center: larger nudge upward (+0.04 to that level, -0.04 from center) — student is stretching successfully
   - Failure above center: tiny nudge back (+0.01 to center, -0.01 from that level) — expected difficulty, not punitive
   - Success below center: no change — expected ease
   - Failure below center: nudge further down (+0.03 to that level, -0.03 from center) — genuine struggle signal
   - Re-normalize to sum 1.0. Levels below 0.05 threshold snap to 0 and redistribute. Update `centerLevel` to whichever level has the highest weight.

2. [ ] [IMP] Wire nudge into session service `respond()`: after grading a response, determine which presentation level was served for this problem (stored in session state or derivable from content query). Call `nudgeDistribution()` and persist the updated distribution to `user_subject_presentation`. Batch writes if needed (update once per session rather than per response for performance).

3. [ ] [IMP] Add distribution history tracking: new `presentation_drift_log` table (`userId, subjectId, fromWeights JSON, toWeights JSON, trigger TEXT, createdAt`). Log each meaningful shift (when centerLevel changes, or when a level drops to 0 or emerges from 0). Not every micro-nudge — only transitions. Useful for debugging and future analytics.

4. [ ] [IMP] Add drift rate configuration: define nudge magnitudes as constants (not hardcoded in the function). Initially fixed across all subjects. Structure so they could be made per-discipline or per-subject later (e.g., context-layered subjects might drift slower because linguistic complexity is more tightly coupled to content depth). Document the constants and rationale.

5. [ ] [TST] Verify: `nudgeDistribution` applies correct deltas for each scenario. Weights always sum to 1.0 after nudge. Levels below threshold snap to 0. Center level updates when highest weight shifts. Simulated sequence: 20 consecutive successes at center level measurably shifts distribution upward. Simulated sequence: 10 consecutive failures shifts downward. Drift log records centerLevel transitions.

**Validation:** A simulated student's distribution drifts from intermediate-centered to standard-centered over ~20-30 successful sessions. Distribution never hard-jumps — transitions are gradual. Drift log captures the progression.
